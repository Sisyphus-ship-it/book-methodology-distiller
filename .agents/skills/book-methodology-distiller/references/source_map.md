## Status Rules

- `full`: source text or user-provided transcript/notes have been inspected sufficiently for source-grounded extraction.
- `partial`: table of contents, selected chapters, excerpts, clips, comments, notes, or partial transcript were inspected.
- `transcript-read`: video/audio transcript, subtitles, or detailed timestamp notes were inspected, but the original media was not necessarily watched.
- `post-read`: article, post, or thread body was inspected.
- `comments-read`: selected comments or audience feedback were inspected.
- `metadata-only`: only title, author/creator, URL, platform metadata, or engagement counts are available.
- `title-only`: only the source name/title is available.
- Mark public-knowledge inference explicitly in `Notes`.
- Never add page numbers, chapter claims, or exact source positions unless they were verified.
- Never claim to have watched a video if only transcript, title, or user notes were available.
- Prefer 3-5 high-quality samples or passages over large sets of weak, noisy materials.
